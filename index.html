About Me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in Electrical Computer Engineering at University of Texas at Austin, under the supervision of Prof. Atlas Wang. I received the B.Eng. and M.S. degree (advised by Prof. Li Niu and Prof. Liqing Zhang) in Computer Science and Technology from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, in 2019 and 2022, respectively.

I was a research intern at Meta Fair with Weiyao Wang, Matt Feiszli, at Snap Research with Jian Wang.

I received MLSys'25 Outstanding Paper Award (Honorable Mention), CVPR'25 AI4CC Workshop Best Paper Award.

Recent Updates

  • Our abstract paper of VideoLifter received the Best Paper Award at CVPR'25 AI4CC Workshop.
  • I joined Adobe as Research Scientist Intern this summer!
  • We are organizing End-to-End 3D Learning workshop at ICCV'25! See you in Hawaii.
  • Our APOLLO received the Outstanding Paper Award (Honorable Mention) at MLSys'25.

    • Research Interests

    My research interests mainly lie in dynamic and complex worlds modeling, including digitalizing the real world and generating a controllable synthetic world. I'm also interested in efficient AI algorithems, including both efficient training and efficient inference of large foundation models.
